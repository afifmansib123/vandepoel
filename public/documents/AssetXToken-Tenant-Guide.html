1. Getting Started as a Tenant

Creating Your Tenant Account:

  1. Visit AssetXToken.com and click "Sign Up"
  2. Select "Tenant" as your account type
  3. Provide basic information:
    • Full name
    • Email address
    • Phone number
    • Current address
  4. Verify your email address
  5. Complete your profile (increases application approval chances):
    • Employment information
    • Annual income
    • References (previous landlords, employers)
    • Pet information (if applicable)
    • Upload ID and income verification documents
üí° Pro Tip: A complete profile significantly increases your chances of application approval. Landlords prefer applicants who provide all information upfront.

3. Submitting Applications

Application Process:

  1. Click "Apply Now" on property details page
  2. Review Application Requirements:
    • Income verification (typically 3x monthly rent)
    • Credit check authorization
    • Employment verification
    • References (2-3 typically required)
    • Government-issued ID
  3. Fill Out Application Form:
    • Personal information
    • Employment details
    • Current/previous addresses
    • Emergency contact
    • Reason for moving
    • Desired move-in date
  4. Upload Required Documents:
    • Pay stubs (last 2-3 months)
    • Bank statements (optional but helpful)
    • Employment verification letter
    • Photo ID (driver's license or passport)
    • Previous landlord reference letter
  5. Pay Application Fee: Typically $30-$75 per application
  6. Submit Application
  7. Wait for Landlord Review: Usually 24-72 hours

Application Status:

  • Pending: Application submitted, awaiting landlord review
  • Under Review: Landlord is actively reviewing your application
  • More Info Requested: Landlord needs additional documents/information
  • Approved: Congratulations! Proceed to contract signing
  • Rejected: Application was not approved (you'll receive a reason)
üí° Application Tips:
  • Apply quickly to competitive properties (first come, first served)
  • Prepare documents in advance for faster applications
  • Be honest on applications - misrepresentation can lead to rejection
  • Follow up 48 hours after applying if you haven't heard back

4. Understanding Contracts

What's in a Rental Contract:

Contract Section What It Covers
Parties Your name, landlord name, property manager (if applicable)
Property Details Address, unit number, parking spot, storage unit
Lease Term Start date, end date, renewal options, early termination clause
Rent Amount Monthly rent, due date, late fee policy, acceptable payment methods
Security Deposit Amount, conditions for return, deductions policy
Utilities Which utilities are included, which are tenant responsibility
Maintenance Landlord vs. tenant responsibilities, emergency procedures
Rules & Policies Pets, smoking, noise, guests, subletting, alterations
Termination Notice period, early termination fees, eviction process

  7. Download signed copy for your records

5. Managing Your Residence

Your Tenant Dashboard:

  • Contract Details: View lease terms, expiration date, renewal options
  • Property Information: Landlord/manager contact, property rules, amenities
  • Payment History: See all rent payments, receipts, upcoming due dates
  • Maintenance Requests: Submit and track repair requests
  • Documents: Access lease, move-in checklist, important notices
  • Notifications: Receive important updates from landlord/manager

Paying Rent:

Payment Methods Available:

  • Auto-Pay (Recommended): Automatic monthly payment on due date
  • Manual Payment: Pay via dashboard using:
    • Bank transfer (ACH)
    • Credit/debit card (may have processing fee)
    • Direct deposit

Payment Schedule:

  • Rent typically due on the 1st of each month
  • Grace period (if any) specified in contract
  • Late fees apply after grace period expires
üí° Pro Tip: Set up auto-pay to never miss a payment. On-time rent payments build a positive rental history for future applications.

6. Requesting Maintenance

How to Submit a Maintenance Request:

  1. Go to your Tenant Dashboard
  2. Click "Request Maintenance"
  3. Fill out the request form:
    • Category: Plumbing, electrical, HVAC, appliances, etc.
    • Priority: Emergency, urgent, routine
    • Description: Detailed explanation of the issue
    • Location: Which room/area
    • Photos: Upload images of the problem
    • Access: When can maintenance enter your unit?
  4. Submit request
  5. Receive confirmation and estimated response time
  6. Track request status in dashboard
  7. Rate service quality after completion

Priority Levels:

Priority Examples Expected Response
Emergency Gas leak, no heat (winter), flooding, broken locks Immediate (within hours)
Urgent No hot water, broken AC (summer), toilet not working Within 24 hours
Routine Leaky faucet, paint touch-up, minor repairs Within 3-7 days
‚ö†Ô∏è Emergency Procedures: For life-threatening emergencies (fire, severe gas leak, break-in), call 911 FIRST, then notify your landlord/property manager immediately.

Tenant vs. Landlord Maintenance Responsibility:

  • Landlord Responsible For:
    • Structural repairs (roof, walls, foundation)
    • Major systems (plumbing, electrical, HVAC)
    • Appliances provided with unit
    • Common area maintenance
    • Pest control (unless caused by tenant)
  • Tenant Responsible For:
    • Light bulb replacement
    • Air filter changes
    • Minor unclogging (preventable issues)
    • Damage caused by tenant negligence
    • Cleaning and general upkeep

7. Payment & Deposits

Security Deposit:

What It Covers:

  • Unpaid rent
  • Property damage beyond normal wear and tear
  • Cleaning costs if unit left excessively dirty
  • Unpaid utilities (if tenant-responsible)

Getting Your Deposit Back:

  • Give proper notice before moving out (typically 30-60 days)
  • Clean the unit thoroughly
  • Repair any damage you caused
  • Remove all personal belongings
  • Return all keys and access devices
  • Schedule move-out inspection with landlord

Deposit Return Timeline: Typically 14-30 days after move-out (varies by state law)

Additional Fees:

Fee Type Typical Amount When Charged
Application Fee $30-$75 One-time, when applying
Pet Deposit/Fee $200-$500 If you have pets
Late Rent Fee $50-$100 or 5% of rent When rent is late
Returned Payment Fee $25-$50 If payment bounces
Key Replacement $25-$100 If you lose keys
