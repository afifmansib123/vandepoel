4. Tokenizing Your Property

What is Property Tokenization?

Property tokenization allows you to convert ownership shares of your property into digital tokens that investors can purchase. This enables you to:

  • Raise capital without traditional bank loans
  • Maintain operational control of the property
  • Share rental income with token holders
  • Access funds for renovations, expansion, or other investments

How to Tokenize Your Property:

  1. Navigate to your property listing
  2. Click "Tokenize Property"
  3. Define tokenization parameters:
    • Total Tokens: Number of tokens to create (e.g., 1,000 tokens)
    • Token Price: Price per token in USD (e.g., $100/token = $100,000 total)
    • Revenue Share: Percentage of rental income to distribute (e.g., 50%)
    • Distribution Frequency: Monthly, quarterly, or annually
    • Minimum Purchase: Minimum tokens per buyer (e.g., 10 tokens)
  4. Review and confirm tokenization
  5. Your tokens appear in the Token Marketplace

Example Tokenization Scenario:

Property Value: $500,000

Tokenization Strategy:

  • Create 5,000 tokens at $50 each (targeting 40% of property value)
  • Raise $250,000 in capital
  • Share 40% of monthly rental income with token holders
  • Keep 60% of rental income plus property appreciation

Result: $250,000 immediate capital + ongoing majority of rental income + full property ownership

üí° Strategic Tip: Start with tokenizing 30-50% of your property value. This gives you substantial capital while maintaining majority income and control.

5. Managing Applications

Application Types:

  1. Tenant Applications: Individuals looking to rent your property
  2. Agent Applications: Property managers offering to manage your property

Reviewing Tenant Applications:

  • View all applications in your Applications Dashboard
  • Review applicant information, employment details, references
  • Actions available:
    • Approve: Move forward with this tenant
    • Reject: Decline the application
    • Request More Info: Ask for additional documentation

6. Working with Agents

Why Work with a Property Manager?

  • Handle day-to-day property management
  • Screen and manage tenants
  • Coordinate maintenance and repairs
  • Collect rent and handle deposits
  • Ensure legal compliance

Assigning an Agent:

  1. Agents apply to manage your property through the marketplace
  2. Review agent profiles, licenses, and experience
  3. Approve the agent application
  4. Agent is automatically assigned to your property
  5. Negotiate management fee (typically 8-12% of monthly rent)

7. Managing Contracts

Once you approve a tenant application, you (or your assigned agent) can create a rental contract:

Creating a Contract:

  1. Go to approved application
  2. Click "Create Contract"
  3. Fill in contract details:
    • Lease start and end dates
    • Monthly rent amount
    • Security deposit
    • Payment due date
    • Late payment penalties
    • House rules and policies
    • Maintenance responsibilities
  4. Send contract to tenant for digital signature
  5. Both parties sign electronically
  6. Contract becomes legally binding

8. Best Practices

‚úÖ Do's:

  • Keep your property listings up-to-date with accurate information
  • Respond to applications within 48 hours
  • Maintain high-quality photos and descriptions
  • Be transparent about property conditions and rules
  • Consider tokenization for properties with stable rental income
  • Distribute token dividends on time to maintain investor trust
  • Keep detailed records of all transactions
  • Communicate clearly with tenants, agents, and investors

‚ùå Don'ts:

  • Don't over-tokenize (keep at least 40-50% ownership for control)
  • Don't ignore maintenance requests
  • Don't discriminate in tenant selection
  • Don't make promises you can't keep to token investors
  • Don't forget to factor in property taxes and insurance in your calculations
