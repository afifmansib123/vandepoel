1. Introduction for Property Managers

What is Your Role?

As a Property Manager (Agent) on AssetXToken, you act as an intermediary between landlords and tenants, handling day-to-day property operations. Your responsibilities include:

  • Marketing properties to attract quality tenants
  • Screening and managing tenant applications
  • Creating and enforcing rental contracts
  • Collecting rent and managing deposits
  • Coordinating maintenance and repairs
  • Ensuring legal compliance
  • Handling tenant issues and conflicts
  • Maximizing occupancy rates and property value

4. Managing Applications

Application Management Workflow:

Once assigned to a property, you'll handle all incoming tenant applications:

  1. Receive Applications:
    • Tenants apply through the property listing
    • You receive notifications for new applications
    • Access all applications in your Manager Dashboard
  2. Screen Applicants:
    • Review application details
    • Verify income (should be 2.5-3x monthly rent)
    • Check employment status
    • Contact references
    • Run credit check (if authorized)
    • Review rental history
  3. Make Decision:
    • Approve: Tenant meets criteria, proceed to contract
    • Request More Info: Need additional documentation
    • Reject: Tenant doesn't meet requirements (must provide reason)
  4. Coordinate with Landlord:
    • Some landlords require final approval
    • Provide recommendation and screening summary
    • Await landlord's decision if required

Screening Criteria Best Practices:

Criteria Standard Why It Matters
Income Verification 3x monthly rent Ensures tenant can afford rent comfortably
Credit Score 600+ (varies) Indicates financial responsibility
Rental History No recent evictions Predicts future tenant behavior
Employment Stable job (6+ months) Reliable income source
References 2+ positive references Third-party verification of reliability
‚ö†Ô∏è Fair Housing Compliance: Always follow Fair Housing laws. Do not discriminate based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, familial status, or disability. Apply screening criteria consistently to all applicants.

5. Creating & Managing Contracts

Contract Creation Process:

  1. Navigate to Approved Application
  2. Click "Create Contract"
  3. Fill Out Contract Details:
    • Parties: Tenant name(s), landlord name, your company as manager
    • Property Details: Address, unit number, parking, storage
    • Lease Term:
      • Start date
      • End date (typically 12 months)
      • Renewal options
      • Early termination clause and fees
    • Financial Terms:
      • Monthly rent amount
      • Rent due date (typically 1st of month)
      • Security deposit amount
      • Pet deposit (if applicable)
      • Late fee structure
      • Payment methods accepted
    • Utilities & Services:
      • Which utilities are included in rent
      • Which utilities are tenant responsibility
      • Trash, water, electricity, gas, internet
    • Maintenance Responsibilities:
      • Landlord/manager responsibilities
      • Tenant responsibilities
      • Emergency contact procedures
    • Rules & Policies:
      • Pet policy
      • Smoking policy
      • Guest/subletting policy
      • Noise/quiet hours
      • Property alterations policy
    • Legal Provisions:
      • Entry notice requirements
      • Eviction procedures
      • Governing law
      • Dispute resolution process
  4. Review with Landlord:
    • Landlord reviews and can request changes
    • Make any necessary edits
    • Get landlord approval
  5. Send to Tenant:
    • Tenant receives contract notification
    • Tenant reviews and can request clarifications
    • Tenant digitally signs
  6. Final Signatures:
    • Landlord signs
    • You sign as property manager
    • Contract becomes legally binding
    • All parties receive signed copy

Contract Management Tips:

  • Use state-specific lease templates that comply with local laws
  • Include all verbal agreements in writing
  • Be clear and specific to avoid future disputes
  • Set up calendar reminders for lease expirations
  • Start renewal conversations 60-90 days before expiration
  • Document all amendments and addendums properly

6. Handling Maintenance Requests

Maintenance Management Workflow:

  1. Receive Request:
    • Tenant submits maintenance request through their dashboard
    • You receive immediate notification (email + dashboard)
    • Review request details, priority, and photos
  2. Assess Priority:
    • Emergency: Immediate action required
    • Urgent: Address within 24 hours
    • Routine: Schedule within 3-7 days
  3. Coordinate Repair:
    • For emergencies: Dispatch on-call contractor immediately
    • For other issues: Schedule appropriate contractor
    • Coordinate access to unit with tenant
    • Provide tenant with estimated timeframe
  4. Approve Costs:
    • Get repair quote from contractor
    • For major expenses: Get landlord approval first
    • For routine maintenance: Proceed within budget limits
  5. Oversee Work:
    • Ensure contractor completes work properly
    • Verify tenant satisfaction
    • Document work done and costs
  6. Close Request:
    • Mark request as completed
    • Process contractor payment
    • Update maintenance records
    • Invoice landlord for costs (per management agreement)

Building Contractor Network:

  • Maintain relationships with reliable contractors:
    • Plumber (emergency and routine)
    • Electrician
    • HVAC technician
    • General handyman
    • Landscaper/snow removal
    • Locksmith
    • Pest control
    • Cleaning service
  • Negotiate preferential rates for recurring work
  • Have backup contractors for each category
  • Vet contractors for licensing, insurance, and quality
üí° Pro Tip: Fast, quality maintenance response is the #1 factor in tenant satisfaction and retention. Happy tenants = lower vacancy = happier landlords = more business for you.

7. Working with Landlords

Your Relationship with Landlords:

As a property manager, you work for the landlord, but you must balance the landlord's interests with tenant rights and legal requirements.

Regular Communication:

Report Type Frequency What to Include
Financial Report Monthly Rent collected, expenses, net income, outstanding balances
Property Status Monthly Occupancy, lease expirations, application activity, maintenance updates
Maintenance Summary Monthly Completed repairs, upcoming maintenance, cost breakdown
Incident Reports As needed Tenant issues, property damage, legal matters, emergencies
Annual Review Yearly Year-over-year performance, recommendations, market analysis

Managing Tokenized Properties:

Special Considerations for Token Properties:

  • Multiple Stakeholders: Landlord retains control, but token holders have financial interest
  • Transparency: Token holders may receive financial reports, so maintain meticulous records
  • Performance Pressure: Strong occupancy and income directly affect token value
  • Communication: May need to provide updates to token holder community
  • Opportunity: Successfully managing tokenized properties can lead to more business

When to Escalate to Landlord:

  • üî¥ Major expenses beyond agreed budget (typically over $500-$1000)
  • üî¥ Structural issues or major repairs
  • üî¥ Tenant lease violations requiring eviction
  • üî¥ Legal matters or disputes
  • üî¥ Insurance claims
  • üî¥ Changes to lease terms or rent increases
  • üî¥ Property sale or refinancing situations
